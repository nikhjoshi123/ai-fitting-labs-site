01. The Owner’s Invisible Bleeding

For a boutique owner, every order is a celebration—until the return mail arrives. You pay for the shipping, you pay for the packaging, and you pay for the marketing to get that customer. When a dress comes back because of a "fitting issue," you don't just lose the profit; you lose the soul of that transaction.

Current inventory gets stuck in transit, your cash flow freezes, and your logistics team spends more time processing "failures" than shipping new dreams. Your website is a beautiful window, but without a fitting room, it’s just a guessing game.

02. The Visitor’s Silent Fear

Your visitor isn't just looking at a price tag; they are looking at their own insecurities. They see a beautiful model, but they don't see themselves. This Visual Gap creates anxiety. They want to buy, they want to feel beautiful, but the fear of "it won't fit me" is stronger than the desire for the clothes.

Shopping should be an escape, not a math problem. When they can't visualize the product on their own body, the emotional connection snaps. They don't leave your site because they don't like the clothes—they leave because they don't trust the outcome.
